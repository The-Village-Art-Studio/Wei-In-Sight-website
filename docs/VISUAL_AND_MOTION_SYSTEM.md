# Visual System and Motion Language

## 1. Visual identity
The design language should feel like:
- a contemporary art space
- a cinematic science-fiction whisper
- a refined personal archive
- a premium but intimate digital environment

## 2. Color system

### Primary background
- near-black
- deep charcoal
- or very deep midnight blue

### Primary accent
- neon pink

### Secondary accent
- white

### Optional atmospheric depth
- restrained magenta haze
- faint violet haze

Do not flood the interface with multiple neon colors.

## 3. Typography system
Use a two-layer typographic approach:
- elegant display face for hero lines and major headings
- modern readable sans-serif for navigation, UI, and body text

Typography should feel:
- artistic
- clean
- premium
- slightly editorial

Avoid:
- gamer-tech fonts
- cliché sci-fi fonts
- ultra-condensed novelty fonts
- overly decorative script fonts

## 4. Texture and depth
The site may use subtle visual depth:
- soft glow
- restrained grain
- atmospheric gradients
- minimal scan/noise texture

Keep texture understated.

## 5. 3D body art direction
The 3D body should be:
- original
- stylized
- wireframe or line-based
- calm and poised
- recognizably human but not literal anatomy-heavy

Avoid:
- stock-looking holograms
- generic tech-demo aesthetics
- medically realistic anatomy visuals
- excessive mesh density that looks noisy

## 6. Motion language
Motion should feel:
- slow
- controlled
- elegant
- confident
- slightly mysterious

Motion is not spectacle.  
Motion is emotional punctuation.

## 7. Motion behaviors by type

### Intro motion
Soft reveal, no abrupt zooms

### Hover motion
Low amplitude, subtle highlight

### Focus motion
Camera glide with clarity and breathing room

### Route transitions
Short, clean, atmospheric continuity

### Page reveal
Editorial fade/slide, not theatrical curtain effects

## 8. Glow behavior
Glow is important but must be restrained.

Use glow for:
- anchor highlights
- active navigation states
- selected submenu nodes
- light atmospheric emphasis

Do not over-bloom the entire site.

## 9. Spacing behavior
Keep layouts spacious.

The site should feel composed, not compressed.
Let content breathe.

## 10. Component principles
All components should share the same visual DNA:
- slightly luminous
- premium but understated
- text-first readability
- clear active/inactive states
- no visual clutter

## 11. Section-specific mood shifts
Each section may subtly shift mood while preserving the core system.

### Sight
More image-led, editorial, gallery-like

### Sound
Rhythmic, atmospheric, frequency-inspired

### Touch
Material, textured, close-up, tactile

### Voice
Minimal, intimate, breath-like

### Dream
Cinematic, narrative, emotionally deep

### Heart
Warm, reflective, personal

### Pulse
Clean, refined, decisive, collector-facing

## 12. Design consistency rule
No section may feel like it belongs to a different website.
Mood can shift.  
Design language cannot fragment.
